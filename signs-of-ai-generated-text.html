Top 5 Signs You're Reading AI-Generated Text

Posted by TextDetectorAI Team

With the rise of generative AI tools like ChatGPT, Bard, and Claude, it's becoming harder to tell if the text you're reading was written by a human or a machine. Here are the top five signs that you're likely reading AI-generated content — and what to do about it.

1. Repetitive Phrasing

AI often repeats words, sentence structures, or even entire ideas. If you notice unnatural repetition, it could be a sign the content was machine-generated.

2. Lack of Personal Experience or Emotion

AI doesn't have feelings or memories. If a text avoids personal stories, opinions, or genuine emotion, it may have been written by a bot.

3. Overuse of Generalizations

Phrases like "In today's world" or "Many people believe" are classic AI fillers. While humans do use generalizations, AI tends to rely on them excessively to sound authoritative.

4. Too Polished or "Safe"

AI-generated writing often avoids strong stances, edgy humor, or controversial takes. It may read like it’s been smoothed over for maximum neutrality.

5. It Sounds Good — But Says Very Little

One major red flag is content that flows well but lacks depth or insight. If you finish reading and realize you didn’t learn anything new, there’s a chance it was AI-generated.
